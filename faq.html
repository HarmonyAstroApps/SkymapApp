Skymap FAQ

Common questions about Skymap, answered.

General

How accurate is Skymap's AR tracking?
Very accurate in clear night sky conditions. Accuracy is typically within 0.5 degrees, which is excellent for identifying constellations and major celestial objects. In areas with heavy light pollution, accuracy may decrease slightly.
Does Skymap work offline?
Yes. All constellation data, astronomical calculations, and celestial object information are downloaded and stored locally on your device. You can use Skymap anywhere — even in remote locations without internet.
Can I identify planets with Skymap?
Absolutely. Skymap tracks all major planets (Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Neptune, Uranus) and Pluto in real-time. It shows their current positions in the sky with precise coordinates.
How many constellations does Skymap cover?
All 88 officially recognized constellations. Skymap includes constellation boundaries, mythology, historical context, and stories for each one.

Stargazing & Usage

Can I use Skymap with my telescope?
Yes. Use Skymap to locate objects in the sky, then view them through your telescope. The app's precise coordinates help you find and track celestial objects quickly.
Is Skymap good for beginners?
Perfect for beginners. Skymap is designed for anyone curious about the sky. No astronomy knowledge needed — just point your phone at the sky and explore.
Does Skymap work in light pollution?
Yes, but visibility is better in darker areas. Skymap's AR overlay helps you locate objects even from cities, though you'll see fewer stars visually in light-polluted areas.

Education

Can teachers use Skymap in the classroom?
Yes. Skymap is perfect for teaching astronomy, space science, and navigation. Its educational content makes it engaging for students of all ages.
